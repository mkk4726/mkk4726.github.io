Toric IOL rotational stability 
- Toric -> 난시 교정

> Rotational stability is key for optimizing postoperative visual outcomes because even a small degree of rotation of a toric intraocular lens (IOL) from its target axis can result in a significant reduction in astigmatic correction.

난시를 교정하기 위해 렌즈마다 난시축이 있는데, 이게 수술 후에 돌아가면 문제가 생긴다.
그래서 의사분들에게 전해 듣기로는 토릭 렌즈의 경우에는 일반 렌즈보다 더 큰 사이즈를 넣는다고 한다.


---

자리 잡는 기간

> The rotation of the toric IOL may decrease when the anterior and posterior capsules fuse. Capsule fusion typically begins at approximately 2 weeks, and minimal IOL rotation is observed after 1 month. [^1]


---

관련 AI 연구들

> Compared with aphakic eyes, the rotation of the TICL in phakic eyes requires consideration of more complex factors. 
> In aphakic eyes, the IOL is implanted into the capsular bag, and structural parameters such as axial length (AL) and anterior chamber depth (ACD) have a greater influence on rotational stability.
> However, TICL is implanted in the ciliary sulcus, and its rotation depends on the surrounding structures. 
> Many patients require higher rotational stability because of operative repositioning. 
> The rotational stability of a TICL can be influenced by preoperative lens power, degree of operative rotation,15 and postoperative hepatic position and vault. [^2]
